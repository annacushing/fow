visiting the
WISSAHICKON VALLEY PARK

Over 1.1 million annual visitors take advantage of the park’s abundant recreational opportunities. More than 50 miles of rugged trails offer hikers, mountain bikers, and equestrians the opportunity to explore the park’s dense forests, open meadows, and shaded creeks.

plan a
VISIT

Hike

The Wissahickon Valley Park has more than 50 miles of hiking trails.

Bike

Many bikers use Forbidden Drive, a flat gravel path with easy grades that is suitable for most types of bicycles.

Run

While runners can be spotted on Wissahickon Valley Park’s upper trails, most prefer Forbidden Drive, the wide, flat gravel trail that parallels the creek for five miles, between Northwestern Avenue and Lincoln Drive.

FAQ

  • HOW DO I GET TO THE PARK?

    Because Wissahickon Valley Park is so large, there is not a single street address for it. There are several entrances with free parking throughout the park, which can be seen on this Google Map.

  • HOW CAN I GET A MAP OF THE PARK?

    FOW publishes a detailed fold-out map of the valley that shows trails, landmarks, entrances, parking, and other points of interest.

  • HOW MANY MILES OF TRAILS ARE THERE IN THE WISSAHICKON?

    Wissahickon Valley Park has approximately 50 miles of trails.
